Date: 2026-05-05T21:33:18Z

=== AWS NitroTPM provisioning blocker ===
First attempt: m7a.large with --enclave-options Enabled=false.
Result: dmesg reported 'No TPM chip found, activating TPM-bypass!'
        no /dev/tpm* device created.

Root cause: AWS Nitro TPM 2.0 requires a custom AMI with
TpmSupport=v2.0 attribute set. Canonical's stock Ubuntu 24.04
amd64 AMI ships with TpmSupport=None (no NitroTPM enablement).
Boot mode is uefi-preferred but the AMI doesn't surface the
vTPM device to the guest.

Workaround for full hardware seal/unseal validation: register-image
from the Canonical snapshot with --tpm-support v2.0 OR use a
Marketplace AMI that ships TPM-enabled. Multi-hour follow-up;
deferred to Phase 7.
